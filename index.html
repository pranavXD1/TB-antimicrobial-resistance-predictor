How well does it work?

Honest caveats
  • Probabilities need external recalibration. Discrimination (AUC) transfers; raw scores are systematically overconfident out-of-distribution. AUC is the trustworthy summary.
  • Streptomycin specificity is capped (~0.70 at 90% sensitivity). Some resistance carries no rpsL/rrs/gidB variant (invisible to any variant model) and gidB is conservatively down-weighted — a genotype–phenotype limit, not a threshold artefact.
  • Operating points are re-fit on external scores, disclosed. Training thresholds don't transfer for the GenTB drugs; thresholds are re-derived leave-one-out at 90% sensitivity.
  • Two cohorts, not universality. Strong evidence of cross-lineage generalisation, not proof for every setting. Predictions never replace phenotypic DST.

Method & data

How the models are built, what they see, and exactly how they were validated. Every input below is public and every external result is regenerable from the pipeline described here.

Training data

The first-line and most second/last-line models are trained on the CRyPTIC panel — 12,288 clinical M. tuberculosis isolates with matched whole-genome sequencing and quantitative, MIC-based phenotypes across 13 antitubercular drugs, assembled as a deliberately resistance-enriched global collection.

Where CRyPTIC signal is sparse (pyrazinamide, streptomycin), per-drug candidate-gene matrices from GenTB supplement training. Phenotypes are binarised to resistant / susceptible per drug, and wild-type isolates are retained as all-zero feature rows so each model learns the true background rate rather than only seeing mutants.

Features & representation

Variants are encoded as position-anchored tokens in two coordinate forms — gene-relative (gene@pos_ref>alt) and genome-position against the H37Rv reference NC_000962.3 — so calls from different variant callers harmonise onto the same feature space.

The space is restricted to ~2,012 candidate-gene variants at established resistance loci — rpoB, katG, inhA/fabG1, embA/embB, pncA, gyrA/gyrB, rrs/rpsL/gid, eis, ethA, Rv0678/atpE/pepQ, rrl/rplC, ddn/fbiC — plus per-gene mutation-burden features. Confining the model to candidate genes keeps it interpretable and portable across cohorts, at the deliberate cost of being blind to resistance mechanisms outside the panel.

Models, calibration & interpretability

One gradient-boosted tree classifier (XGBoost) is trained independently per drug. Raw scores are mapped to calibrated probabilities so that a reported 0.9 means roughly what it says on held-out data.

Two operating modes expose the sensitivity–specificity trade-off: Balanced (≥ 0.50) maximises overall accuracy; High-sensitivity (≥ 0.10) minimises missed resistance at the cost of more false positives. Every prediction carries per-variant SHAP attributions — which tokens pushed the call, whether each was present or absent, and the signed size of its contribution.

External-validation pipeline
  1. 1 Pull raw sequencing reads for an independent cohort from the ENA.
  2. 2 Call variants with TB-Profiler against H37Rv (NC_000962.3).
  3. 3 Harmonise the called variants into the CRyPTIC candidate-gene token space.
  4. 4 Score with the frozen model weights — no retraining, ever.
  5. 5 Re-derive decision thresholds on the external calibrated scores by leave-one-out CV — the training thresholds don't transfer for the GenTB drugs.
  6. 6 Leakage check: verify zero overlap by ENA accession where archives are comparable, and by dataset provenance where they are not.
Validation cohorts

Sierra Leone — PRJEB7727 (Schleusener et al., 2017), ~89 usable isolates, West-African lineage 4 with a substantial M. africanum (L5/6) fraction. Those lineage-specific SNPs reduce token overlap with the L2/L4-dominated training set — a documented biological limitation, not a pipeline error.

Belarus — PRJNA200335 (Wollenberg et al., 2017), ~97 isolates, Beijing / lineage 2, a very high-MDR setting. An independent Broad deposit (Minsk, 2010–2013), disjoint from CRyPTIC's prospective 2017–2021 sequencing.

Pooled: n = 186, deliberately spanning opposite ends of the lineage and resistance spectrum.

How to read the numbers

AUC — threshold-free discrimination: the probability the model ranks a random resistant isolate above a random susceptible one. It's the headline because it transfers across cohorts.

Sensitivity / specificity @90% — the operating point that catches 90% of true resistance, and the specificity you pay for it.

95% CIs — bootstrap over pooled isolates (500 resamples).

Calibration caveat — discrimination transfers, but absolute probabilities drift out-of-distribution (systematically overconfident), so scores should be recalibrated per setting before any clinical-style thresholding.

Reproducibility & data availability

All inputs are public: CRyPTIC (ENA/EBI), GenTB, and the validation cohorts PRJEB7727, PRJNA200335, and PRJEB59824 (staged).

Code and small derived artifacts live on GitHub; the large frozen models and the DuckDB feature store are archived on Zenodo with a citable DOI. Every external result on the How it works tab is regenerable end-to-end from the six-step pipeline above.
